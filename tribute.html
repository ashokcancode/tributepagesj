The Father of the iPhone - STEVE JOBS

Steven Paul Jobs (February 24, 1955 – October 5, 2011) was an American businessman, inventor, and investor best known for co-founding the technology giant Apple Inc. Jobs was also the founder of NeXT and chairman and majority shareholder of Pixar. He was a pioneer of the personal computer revolution of the 1970s and 1980s, along with his early business partner and fellow Apple co-founder Steve Wozniak.Jobs was born in San Francisco in 1955 and adopted shortly afterwards. He attended Reed College in 1972 before withdrawing that same year. In 1974, he traveled through India, seeking enlightenment before later studying Zen Buddhism. He and Wozniak co-founded Apple in 1976 to further develop and sell Wozniak's Apple I personal computer. Together, the duo gained fame and wealth a year later with production and sale of the Apple II, one of the first highly successful mass-produced microcomputers. Jobs saw the commercial potential of the Xerox Alto in 1979, which was mouse-driven and had a graphical user interface (GUI). This led to the development of the unsuccessful Apple Lisa in 1983, followed by the breakthrough Macintosh in 1984, the first mass-produced computer with a GUI. The Macintosh introduced the desktop publishing industry in 1985 with the addition of the Apple LaserWriter, the first laser printer to feature vector graphics.

Timeline of His Life

• 1983: Jobs recruited John Sculley from PepsiCo to become Apple's CEO. This decision eventually led to tension and Jobs' departure from Apple.

• 1985: Jobs resigned from Apple after a power struggle with the board of directors. He then founded NeXT Computer and purchased The Graphics Group, which later became Pixar Animation Studios.

• 1986: Jobs bought The Graphics Group for $5 million, which was later renamed Pixar. The studio went on to create hit animated films like Toy Story, Finding Nemo, and more.

• 1996: Apple acquired NeXT Computer for $429 million, bringing Steve Jobs back to the company.

• 1997: Steve Jobs became the interim CEO of Apple, succeeding Gil Amelio.

• 1998: Apple introduced the colorful iMac G3, marking the beginning of a successful era for the company.

• 2001: The first iPod was introduced, revolutionizing the music industry.

• 2003: Apple launched the iTunes Store, making it easy for users to legally purchase and download music.

• 2007: The first iPhone was introduced, changing the landscape of the mobile phone industry.

• 2008: Apple introduced the App Store, creating a platform for third-party app developers.

• 2010: The first iPad was launched, popularizing the tablet market.

• 2011: Steve Jobs resigned as CEO of Apple due to health issues. He passed away on October 5, 2011, at the age of 56, after a long battle with pancreatic cancer.
